DID Traits

DID Traits is a groundbreaking specification developed by the Decentralized Identity Foundation that provides a systematic approach to understanding and comparing Decentralized Identifier (DID) methods. The standardization offers a comprehensive framework for evaluating the unique characteristics of different DID implementations.

The challenge

With the rapid evolution of decentralized identity technologies, developers and organizations face a complex landscape of DID methods. Each method has distinct capabilities, making it challenging to select the most appropriate solution for specific use cases.

Solution: A comprehensive trait framework

The DID Traits specification introduces:

  • Detailed Trait Definitions: A systematic catalog of measurable characteristics that distinguish DID methods
  • Machine-Readable Schema: A JSON schema enabling precise, structured representation of DID method capabilities
  • Comprehensive Comparison: A detailed matrix comparing traits across multiple DID methods

Benefits

For developers

  • Simplified DID method selection
  • Clear, standardized method comparison
  • Insights into technical capabilities

For organizations

  • Enhanced decision-making for identity infrastructure
  • Comprehensive understanding of DID method strengths
  • Future-proof identity solutions
